Q1 Parents and Q1 Kids and their Mean Rank earning and the probability for the success of the child

Analysis

The x axis on this graph represents the following value: par_q1: Fraction of parents in an income quintile 1 (which is the lowest) k_q1: Fraction of kids in an income quintile 1 (which is the lowest) k_rank_cond_parq1: Mean kid earnings rank conditional on parent in quintile 1 ktop1pc_cond_parq1: Probability of kid in top 1% conditional on parent in quintile 1 and on the other hand, the y axis was represented the ic level, which would reperesent whether it was a two year or a four year college. The zero values although minimal, have been placed on the graph as the other category. The reason I choose this specific data set, is because I was very much intersted in having a look at how the low income families are able to have their kids succeed with a college education. The University that I attend, provides a lot of financial aid to those who need it and its important to see if a 4 year degree, makes it worth it for those young adults to have some success. It is clear, that the parents in quartile one have their kids attend mostly two year colleges initally, and not have them.
